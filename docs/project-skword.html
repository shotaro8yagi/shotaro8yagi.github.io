Organizational Development (SKword)

2022–2025 (ongoing) · Lead Producer / Project Coach

Overview

SKword is a creative design firm in Japan of around 70 people. Their existing way of running projects was mostly waterfall and task-based, managed through Gantt charts. This approach made it difficult to respond to clients’ emerging and ambiguous messages, or to hold space for exploration as those messages evolved. At the same time, the firm wanted more members who could independently manage and produce projects, rather than relying on a few key individuals.

This project began as a change initiative led in partnership with the CEO, who had been a participant in my 8-week project management bootcamp. Our shared aim was to redesign project practice so that SKword could respond more flexibly to client needs and grow internal project capability across the team.

Process

  • Formed a cross-functional team bringing together members from different departments to share and surface their existing project practices.
  • Guided the team through experiential learning using Project Sprint, combined with lectures and coaching on project design and facilitation.
  • Supported the team in co-developing their own method of project practice that better matched SKword’s context and client work.
  • Helped members apply, practice, and track the new method in live projects to test, refine, and normalize it.
  • Later, helped establish a PMO-style team to intervene in projects, support adoption, and improve performance across the organization.

Outcome

  • The new project practice was documented, standardized, and shared internally as SKword’s own way of working.
  • Approximately 80% of employees were exposed to the new practice through workshops, interventions, or direct project involvement.
  • The organization increased its internal capacity for project management, reducing reliance on a small number of project leads.
