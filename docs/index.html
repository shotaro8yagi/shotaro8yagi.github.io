Practice Design & Sociology

Hi, I’m Shotaro.

I work with teams and organisations to change how they work.

My work sits between project practice, self-organization, and ontology. I’m interested in how outputs reshape thinking, how accidents change forms, and how teams become more autonomous through practice.

I’m currently based between Glasgow and Tokyo, and I’m always happy to talk with people who are working on something messy, geeky, or new.

Research

My research looks at how people collaborate around things they do not yet fully understand.

In my PhD, I’m currently exploring hacker's practice to understand how material-practices self-organize.

Work and Creation

If your work isn't working, count me in! I love people struggling to pull off difficult work, not because it is necessary but because it is meaningful.

Most of my work involves helping teams notice and reshape how they work while they are working.

This includes designing output-led project practices, facilitating reflection, building shared language, and supporting teams as they experiment with new ways of organising themselves.
